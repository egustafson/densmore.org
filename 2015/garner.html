Some Flying Experiences

1943 - 1973

by: Vernon W. Garner, Lt. Col. USAF (retired)

Enroute to N. Korea target - 1951 (B-29)

Background and download link

About a year ago (as this is written in January 2015) I visited my Mother at her home in Solomons, as I try to do as frequently as possible despite that we live in Colorado. Her home is part of a collection of apartments where care is graduated depending on need, and it caters to our senior citizens. I often ponder the stories her neighbors have to tell.

During our visit, we shared a meal with a couple whose company I greatly enjoyed. In particular, the gentleman had been "prepped" by my Mom that I was interested in WWII aviation. He did have a wealth of stories. But he did better than that! He gave me a copy of some of the stories he has written for the Asbury Solomons newsletter. I asked Mr. Garner if it would be okay for me to post this booklet on the internet, and he said that would be very nice. I certainly think so - it honors our greatest generation.

Therefore, I have scanned in this booklet of some 15 pages for y'all. My scanner software isn't known for its compaction algorithms, the booklet in pdf is over 13 megabytes, so apologies if it takes a minute to download. Please read on, and let me know if you enjoyed it.
