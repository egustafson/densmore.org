Antique Airplane Association Fly-in

Labor Day Weekend, 2012    
    Movies       Photos    

Hi folks! I have movies and I have photos. The movies aren't too long so I should put them up on youTube but I have not yet done that.

I have over 300 photos that I worked through. In the end I have posted 136 here; probably too many and I'll bore you, but the JAlbum photo presenter makes it easy to look at thumbs until you see one you'd like to see larger. As you will see, I tended to focus on these topics:

  • A few airplanes with which I was completely enthralled (Bleriot, Trimotor, Boeing 40C)
  • People operating their aircraft
  • Unusual elements - the moonshine (not the liquor variety, though I did get to taste some!), rubber chickens, landing lights, etc.

A few of the photos have received very minor photoshop treatment - a crop here, a little more contrast there (cloudy days suck for photography!). Most of them, however, are not retouched. I hope you enjoy my images even half as much as I enjoyed the thrill of being there and taking them, and seeing all your gorgeous airplanes.

Below is a photo of Eric Presten's 1909 Bleriot. It's a faithful replica though constructed of modern materials. The engine is a 1930s radial - the original engine just isn't very safe. This is the first time a wing warping aircraft has ever operated from Antique Aerodrome. Eric didn't just hop it either. In the very early morning, still air, he took it around the pattern! It was a wonderful privilege to see a piece of history like this fly.
